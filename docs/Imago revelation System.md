# Imago Revelation & Unlock System

## Overview

Two actions power the Imago research system:

- **Explore Beliefs** — an ongoing multi-tick action that accumulates *Revelation* in a targeted Domain's pool
- **Reveal Imago** — a one-time action that spends Revelation from a Domain pool to unlock a specific Imago

### Reveal Imago action

Reveal Imago is to be added as an action in the Self-Refinement category, the same category that Explore Beliefs is in.

When selected, a Domain selection modal is shown; when a Domain is selected, an Imago selection modal is then shown.

This screen shows the current Revelation costs of the Imagines in the tree, with an additional field at the top showing the amount of Revelation accumulated for that Domain in total.

When an Imago is selected here, the description and mechanical details of the Imago are shown (much like when the player is making use of the Imago in another context). The confirmation button here is labeled "Reveal"; when selected, if Revelation is not sufficient or prerequisites are not yet met, an error toast is shown stating as such and clarifying either how much Revelation is needed and how much the player currently lacks or what needs to be unlocked first; when selected and if the Imago is eligible for Revelation, the player is asked to confirm if they want to spend their Revelation (and clarifying how much) before a "yes" response unlocks the selected Imago, adding it to `state.demiurge.unlocked_imagines` and a new `state.demiurge.revealed_imagines`.

Both of these are sufficient for (a) allowing the new Imago to be used by the player with other actions and (b) feeding into calculating the cost malus (detailed below) that increases the overall costs of Imagines after more have been Revealed.

When a Domain contains an Imago that is eligible for Revelation, its border on the Domain selection modal is brighter. Similarly, any eligible Imago also has a brighter border on the Imago selection modal.

Imagines are organized into **Trees** of 7 Imagines per Domain (Tier 1 × 2, Tier 2 × 2, Tier 3 × 2, Tier 4 × 1). The player starts with a number of Imagines (typically 4) already unlocked, spread among the Tier-1 Imagines in their starting affiliated Domains.

## Revelation Generation (Explore Beliefs)

### Rate Formula

Each tick that Explore Beliefs is running on a Domain, the Demiurge accumulates:

```
revelation_per_tick = base_rate
                    + (affinity_bonus if Domain is currently affiliated else 0)
                    + floor(universal_expression × 3)
```

Where:
- **`base_rate`** = 10 Revelation/tick
- **`affinity_bonus`** = +7 Revelation/tick (flat addition; the Domain must be one of the Demiurge's four current affiliated Domains)
- **`universal_expression`** = the Domain's current expression value across the universe (which we will create an expression to derive from the Essence generated by this Domain and normalized to a value between 0.1 and 1.0, where what constitutes "full expression" will correspond to a value that we will have to experiment with to hone in on); contribution is minor by design (max +3/tick at full expression)

This gives:
- **Minimum rate** (no affinity, baseline 0.1 expression): **~10/tick**
- **Maximum rate** (affinity + full expression): **20/tick**

To cut down on float memory glut, before being added to the total Revelation for the Domain or displayed to the player as a delta, these values will be cut off to the nearest hundredth.

The total Revelation accumulated for each Domain will be stored in a dict in a new attribute we will call `state.demiurge.revelation_pools`, where each entry is `{"domain_name", float}`, e.g., `{"domain:order", 25.68}`.  It would be a good idea to set the default for the dict in this attribute to entries with all 16 Domains with 0.0 Revelation each.

### Key design notes

- The affinity bonus is re-evaluated **every tick**, not at action start. Switching affiliated Domains mid-research takes effect immediately.
- The expression modifier is intentionally small so players are not heavily penalized for researching Domains with low universal expression, which will often be necessary to address raising that Domain expression in the first place.
- The affinity bonus creates a meaningful strategic choice: switch affiliations to research faster, or hold unrelated affiliated Domains for better Essence income.

### Proxius Research

We will add an action to the Proxius Direction category to direct a Proxius to perform Domain research. (I am not yet sure on a name for this action, so we will have to brainstorm that or go with a placeholder for now.) This will instantiate an Agent attached to the Proxius with the goal of accumulating Revelation in that Domain. Depending on their loyalty, they may perform an action to do so every tick or just every so often; reference the Issue Directive action for guidance on how to structure this.

When selecting this new action, a Domain selection modal is used, similar to how it appears when using the Explore Beliefs action.

A Proxius performing research on behalf of the Demiurge generates, as a base, **2 Revelation/tick**, with:

- up to a +2 bonus applied depending on the relevant Domain expression in the Significant Location (e.g. planet) where the Proxius currently is; and
- a flat +1 bonus applied if the Proxius's beliefs include that Domain with at least a 0.4 affinity.

This lower base rate and lower max rate reflect the Demiurge's divine nature (incorporeal mind, unrestricted information access, superior supernatural capacity) versus mortal limitations. Directional, background research by a Proxius is meaningful but never a substitute for the Demiurge's direct focus.

Revelation from this Proxius activity is funneled directly to the Demiurge's Domain Revelation pools and does not require any additional action to "acquire."

*Future: the Demiurge's Stronghold can include a Cosmic Library that significantly boosts Proxius research rates.*

## Revelation Costs (Reveal Imago)

### Base Costs by Tier

| Tier | Base Revelation Cost |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 | 60 |
| Tier 2 | 100 |
| Tier 3 | 200 |
| Tier 4 | 400 |

### Malus: Scaling Cost Increase

As the player reveals more Imagines beyond the ones they start with, all Revelation costs increase slightly. This discourages breadth-first play and ensures late-game research feels like a meaningful investment.

```
adjusted_cost = floor(base_cost × (1 + 0.003 × state.demiurge.revealed_imagines))
```

Where **`state.demiurge.revealed_imagines`** = total Imagines revealed via Reveal Imago (does **not** include the starting Imagines).

**Properties of the malus:**
- At `revealed_imagines` = 0–5: rounding eliminates any effect on Tier-1 costs entirely
- At `revealed_imagines` = 6: Tier-1 costs increase by 1 point for the first time
- At `revealed_imagines` = 101 (every other tree complete): ~30% cost increase across all tiers
- The malus is evaluated at the moment of each individual Reveal Imago action, so revealing Imagines in other trees while researching the current one incrementally raises costs for this tree's remaining Imagines

### Expected timelines at minimum rate (10/tick)

| Tier | Base cost | Ticks (no malus) | Ticks (101 extra revealed) |
|---|---|---|---|
| T1 | 60 | 6 | 8 |
| T2 | 100 | 10 | 13–14 |
| T3 | 200 | 20 | 26–27 |
| T4 | 400 | 40 | 53 |

### Full tree at maximum late-game malus (101 revealed, min rate)

Completing the final tree after having revealed every other tree's Imagines:

| Imago | Extra at unlock | Cost | Ticks |
|---|---|---|---|
| T1 Left | 101 | 78 | 8 |
| T1 Right | 102 | 78 | 8 |
| T2 Left | 103 | 130 | 13 |
| T2 Right | 104 | 131 | 14 |
| T3 Left | 105 | 263 | 27 |
| T3 Right | 106 | 263 | 27 |
| T4 | 107 | 528 | 53 |
| **Total** | | **1,471** | **150** |

Compared to completing the same tree first (extra = 0, min rate): 1,120 Revelation, 112 ticks. The late-game overhead is ~34% — meaningful but not punishing.

## Prerequisites

Each Imago in a tree has prerequisites that must be revealed first:

- **either Tier 1**: No prerequisites
- **either Tier 2**: Requires either T1
- **Tier 3 Left**: Requires T2 Left; **Tier 3 Right**: Requires T2 Right
- **Tier 4**: Requires **all** T1, T2, and T3 Imagines

(These prerequisites are already present in `core/core.db`.)

### Revelation order does not matter strategically

Because the malus applies uniformly to all costs regardless of which Imago is being unlocked, the order in which a player reveals Imagines within a tree has negligible effect on total cost (typically ≤1 Revelation difference). Players should not feel compelled to optimize reveal order.

## The Revelation Pool

### One pool per Domain

Each Domain has its own Revelation pool that accumulates independently. Switching Explore Beliefs to a different Domain starts accumulating in that Domain's pool without affecting others.

### Dynamic cap

The Revelation pool for each Domain is capped at:

```
cap = sum of adjusted_cost for all unrevealed Imagines in this Domain's tree
```

This cap is recalculated each tick using the current malus value. It is **not** a hardcoded worst-case ceiling.

**Why this matters:** If a player accumulates the maximum Revelation in a tree but leaves it unspent, and then they reveal Imagines in other trees (raising the malus), the cap for the original Domain adjusts upward accordingly—but not their accumulated Revelation. When returning to the original Domain tree, the player may have to do a small amount of additional research to reach the new cap if it has meaningfully increased. This is intentional — Revelation is meant to be spent, not banked. Sitting on it while the malus rises is its own penalty.

If you have already exceeded a Domain's current Revelation cap or you have already unlocked every Imago in its tree, you get an error toast message if you try to use Explore Beliefs on that Domain (or when directing a Proxius to research it) and cannot complete the action.

### Auto-stop behavior

Explore Beliefs automatically stops when `pool ≥ cap` — i.e., when the player has accumulated enough Revelation to purchase every remaining Imago in the tree. There is no benefit to continuing research beyond this point.

Explore Beliefs does **not** stop when intermediate Imagines become individually affordable. The player may be mid-sprint toward a specific Tier-3 Imago and intentionally passing on cheaper Imagines along the way. Interrupting the action in that case would be an annoyance, not a feature.

When you have unlocked every Imago in a Domain's tree and if you also have an ongoing Explore Belief action on that Domain, the action is immediately canceled. Any Proxius Agent that was engaged in research on that Domain will similarly cease acting, and the Agent will be removed on the next tick. (This would be a good opportunity to workshop a sort of "mission complete" log notification from a Proxius asking for their next directive.)

## Notifications

- Each tick that Explore Beliefs resolves, a message reports progress (Revelation gained, current pool total, current Domain cap)
- When the pool reaches or exceeds the cost of any **currently unlockable** Imago (prerequisites met), a notification is generated: *"You have enough Revelation in [Domain] to unlock [Imago]."*
- These notifications are informational only — they do not pause or cancel the ongoing action.
- When the pool hits the cap (all Imagines fundable), Explore Beliefs stops and a final notification is generated.

When the full Notification system is in place (and not just tick-by-tick logs), notifications concerning the Explore Beliefs action and Imago Revelation will be very important parts of it.

## Action Interruption

The existing action interruption system already handles this cleanly. When the player selects the Self-Refinement action category while having an ongoing Explore Beliefs running, they are presented with options to: cancel the ongoing action, pause it for one tick to take a different action, or leave it running. It will be a natural, common occurrence to pause Explore Beliefs to take a one-tick Reveal Imago action. Because this is how action categories and ongoing actions work in general, no special handling is required for the Revelation system.

## Summary of Intended Playstyle

The system is designed to reward **focused, directed research** rather than breadth-first accumulation:

- Researching affiliated Domains is faster but competes with Essence strategy
- Revealing Imagines promptly as you have the Domain Revelation keeps the malus from raising costs unnecessarily
- Steamrolling one branch of a tree (T1 → T2 → T3 on one side before touching the other) is a valid strategy and is not penalized by any action interruption mechanics
- The Tier-4 capstone is a genuine late commitment: it requires the full tree to be revealed first and significant dedicated research time on top of that
