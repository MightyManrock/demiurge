# Water

**Thematic arc:** Flow and adaptive persistence → Patient erosion and indiscriminate nourishment → The flood that destroys to renew and the depths that preserve → The Primordial.

## Tier 1

### The Steady Current

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The current doesn't stop because there is something in the way.*

**Description:** Water is ceaseless, incorporating any obstacles into itself and becoming something other than what it was in the process. This compromise is inherent in the river.

**Mechanics:** `water:0.35, sacrifice:0.1, order:-0.1, nomadism:0.1, patience:0.2, adaptability:0.15`

### The Still Depths

**Tooltip Blurb:** *There is more below the surface than the surface suggests—and always has been.*

**Description:** Water in its calmest state has nothing to say, no truth to reveal. In the deepest of depths lies the most humbling solitude; in bearing the weight if itself there is little meaning. 

**Mechanics:** `water:0.3, silence:0.1, truth:-0.1, isolationism:0.15, humility:0.2`

## Tier 2

### The Patient River

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The stone does not break because the water is angry but because it arrived every day for millennia.*

**Description:** The river does not merely pass through the landscape but writes itself into the landscape, and what it writes cannot be unwritten. What the river keeps is not the memory of struggle but the shape of where it has been, carved into the stone like a record that cannot be disputed.

**Mechanics:** `water:0.55, memory:0.15, conflict:-0.05, conservationism:0.15, patience:0.2, tenacity:0.15`

## The Fostering Rain

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The rain does not ask whose land it falls on but simply falls.*

**Description:** The rain falls on every surface equally—on the wicked and the righteous, the prepared and the unprepared. Whether it is divine justice or divine indifference, nourishment freely given asks nothing in return and means nothing beyond itself, and that is the truest generosity.

**Mechanics:** `water:0.5, community:0.12, secrecy:-0.04, cooperation:0.15, egalitarianism:0.2, adaptability:0.15`

## Tier 3 — Left (requires The Patient River)

### The Great Flood

**Tooltip Blurb**: *What the flood leaves behind is richer than what it found, though this may be little comfort.*

**Description:** The flood does not distinguish between what deserves to be destroyed and what does not. What remains after may be richer for the destruction, but those who suffered it remember what the gods were doing while the water rose.

**Mechanics:** `water:0.8, decay:0.45, order:-0.02, maltheism:0.1, adaptability:0.3, tenacity:0.2, folk_wisdom:0.15`

## Tier 3 — Right (requires The Fostering Rain)

### The Sunken Archive

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The sea keeps what the land cannot, including what may have been better lost.*

**Description:** The sea preserves without curation, holding the record of what was built and what was lost with equal fidelity. What lies at the bottom has been kept from decay not out of mercy but because water, in sufficient depth, is indifferent to time.

**Mechanics:** `water:0.75, truth:0.4, fire:-0.02, conservationism:0.2, erudition:0.2, patience:0.2, humility:0.1`

## Tier 4 (requires all previous)

### The Primordial Sea

**Tooltip Blurb:** *Water is the source from which all flow originates and to which all flow returns.*

**Description:** The divine concept of Water itself.

**Mechanics:** `water:1.0`
