# Growth

**Thematic arc:** The first emergence and the reliable return → Wild abundance and patient cultivation → The overwhelming ancient and the organism of civilization → The Verdant Absolute.

## Tier 1

### The Eager Seedling

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The seed knows what it is going to become and surrenders its current form without hesitation.*

**Description:** Growth begins with what looks like self-destruction: the seed cracks open, surrendering the only form it has ever known in exchange for the form it will become. Nothing in the seedling hesitates; it has no attachment to what it is giving up.

**Mechanics:** `growth:0.35, sacrifice:0.1, memory:-0.1, animism:0.2, idealism:0.15`

### The Cycle of Return

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The spring does not remember the winter was difficult, but it returns anyway.*

**Description:** The seasons do not require memory or intention to keep their promise; spring arrives because that is what spring does. The world that expects the return and plants accordingly is practicing the oldest and most reliable form of faith.

**Mechanics**: `growth:0.3, truth:0.1, conflict:-0.1, agriculture:0.2, humility:0.15`

## Tier 2

### The Wild Garden

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The garden that tends itself does not require permission, whether to persist or to be built over.*

**Description:** Life reclaims what was taken from it with patience and without malice; the garden built over will simply wait. What grows without direction tends to be harder to remove than what was planted with it.

**Mechanics:** `growth:0.55, fire:0.15, order:-0.05, animism:0.2, adaptability:0.2, folk_wisdom:0.15`

### The Cultivated Field

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The field that yields abundantly is not generous; it is the farmer who is generous with what he has learned to ask of it.*

**Description:** Agriculture is the long negotiation between the will of sapient mortals and the stubbornness of living things, requiring more tenacity than strength and more observation than command. The farmer who has learned what the soil asks of them understands growth more honestly than any doctrine can.

**Mechanics:** `growth:0.5, mastery:0.12, silence:-0.04, agriculture:0.15, sedentism:0.1, tenacity:0.2, pragmatism:0.15`

## Tier 3 — Left (requires The Wild Garden)

### The Ancient Forest

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The forest was here before the town walls and will be here after.*

**Description:** The forest operates on a timescale that makes mortal history look like a brief disturbance in the canopy. To walk beneath trees older than your civilization's name is a specific kind of instruction in humility.

**Mechanics:** `growth:0.8, memory:0.45, mastery:-0.02, animism:0.5, conservationism:0.2, humility:0.2, patience:0.1`

## Tier 3 — Right (requires The Cultivated Field)

### The Living City

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The city grows the way living things grow: by consuming, by competing, by making room for what is next.*

**Description**: The city that stops consuming, competing, and making room is no longer a city but a ruin that has not yet fallen. Every generation of inhabitants inherits a place shaped by those before them and reshapes it for those who come after, whether they intend to or not.

**Mechanics:** `growth:0.75, conflict:0.4, secrecy:-0.02, sedentism:0.2, commerce:0.15, ambition:0.2, tenacity:0.2, pragmatism:0.1`

## Tier 4 (requires all previous)

### The Verdant Absolute

**Tooltip Blurb:** *Growth is the fundamental condition of flourishing, the irreducible vitality underlying all living things.*

**Description:** The divine concept of Growth itself.

**Mechanics:** `growth:1.0`
