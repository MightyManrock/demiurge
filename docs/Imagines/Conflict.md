# Conflict

## Tier 1

### The Broken Banner

**Tooltip Blurb:** *You forget what you've lost so you can keep fighting.*

**Description:** The banner is broken because someone carried it past the point of hope; sacrifice is the hidden core of defiance. And conflict sustains itself by forgetting; those who fight on against impossible odds do so by erasing the memory of what the war has already cost them.

**Mechanics:** `conflict:0.35, sacrifice:0.1, memory:-0.1, competition:0.15, tenacity:0.2, sincerity:0.1`

### The Rival's Eye

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The water finds a way around; the rival breaks through.*

**Description:** Rivalry drives discovery; you learn your own limits by pushing against another's. But the rival's eye despises yielding; it has no patience for the flowing, accommodating path that goes around rather than through.

**Mechanics:** `conflict:0.3, mastery:0.08, water:-0.1, competition:0.2, ambition:0.2`

## Tier 2

### The Sharpened Edge

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The fighter carries every wound into the next battle.*

**Description:** Mastery of violence requires memory; technique is encoded experience, and the trained warrior carries the memory of every mistake into the next engagement. But the martial life stunts organic development; the blade that is sharpened cannot also grow.

**Mechanics:** `conflict:0.55, memory:0.15, growth:-0.04, conquest:0.15, hierarchy:0.1, tenacity:0.2, pragmatism:0.15`

### The Contested Ground

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The emptiness is what makes the ground worth dying for.*

**Description:** The contested ground is defined by what it lacks: the void that power rushes to fill, the territory that exists as absence until someone dies for it. Strangely, the violence of contestation preserves what it fights over; the fought-over field resists decay.

**Mechanics:** `conflict:0.5, void:0.12, decay:-0.05, nomadism:0.1, competition:0.15, ambition:0.2, tenacity:0.1`

## Tier 3 — Left *(requires The Sharpened Edge)*

### The Eternal War

**Tooltip Blurb:** *Wars last forever because people remember.*

**Description:** The eternal war is sustained by memory—every grievance, every fallen comrade, every broken promise—kept alive across generations to fuel the next battle. Memory is the eternal war's true weapon. But the eternal war has no room for growth; it only has room for more war.

**Mechanics:** `conflict:0.85, memory:0.4, growth:-0.02, conquest:0.5, xenophobia:0.15, tenacity:0.25, folk_wisdom:0.15`

## Tier 3 — Right *(requires The Contested Ground)*

### The Crucible of Becoming

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The sword does not yield; neither does what is made by the sword.*

**Description:** The crucible destroys in order to enable growth, the non-obvious truth that devastation and flourishing share a root. What survives is genuinely new. The crucible does not flow around obstacles; it burns through them.

**Mechanics:** `conflict:0.75, growth:0.45, water:-0.02, competition:0.45, hierarchy:0.15, ambition:0.25, pragmatism:0.15`
