# Truth

**Thematic arc:** Reliable orientation and blinding clarity → The devoted truth-seeker and the social oath → Truth as weapon or Truth as foundation of civilization → The Absolute.

## Tier 1

### The Weathered Compass

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The compass that has survived everything still points true. That is not a small thing.*

**Description:** Truth is the basis of reliable navigation—practical, hard-won, earned through use rather than revelation. The instrument endures.

**Mechanics:** `truth:0.35, water:0.1, void:-0.1, honesty:0.2, patience:0.15`

## The Glint of Truth

**Tooltip Blurb:** *You cannot unknow what you have seen. This is both the gift and the cost.*

**Description:** Revelation is the blinding moment when something is fully and finally known. Truth as exposure is not concerned with comfort.

**Mechanics:** `truth:0.35, sacrifice:0.1, change:-0.1, honesty:0.2, sincerity:0.15`

## Tier 2

### The Haggard Sage

**Tooltip Blurb:** *Wisdom is not given. It is what remains after everything else has been taken.*

**Description:** Devoted totally to truth-seeking, the philosopher has surrendered comfort, community, and certainty in pursuit of understanding, earning wisdom through loss.

**Mechanics:** `truth:0.55, decay:0.15, community:-0.05, erudition:0.2, patience:0.2, humility:0.1`

### The Sworn Witness

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The witness who tells the truth when it costs them nothing is not a witness but a bystander who happened to speak.*

**Description:** The social contract, the testimony, the record, the oath that binds—truth is what makes collective life possible.

**Mechanics:** `truth:0.5, community:0.12, secrecy:-0.04, honesty:0.2, sincerity:0.15, cooperation:0.15`

### The Brutal Reckoning

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The account does not care whether you are ready for it.*

**Description:** Truth as a weapon is the tribunal, the exposure, the verdict that cannot be appealed. This is truth in service of judgment rather than understanding, the revelation that destroys what it illuminates.
