Domains, being transcendental ur-concepts that mortal minds do not readily grasp, each have a "tech tree" of "mortal-friendly" abstract ideas—these are the primary tools that a Demiurge and their Proxii use to "incept" domain alignment into mortal individuals and civilizations, thereby increasing domain expression in the universe. These abstract ideas are called Imagines (sing. Imago).

Except for the most advanced Imagines, an Imago is not a "pure" expression of a domain, including "rider effects" such as traces of influence from another domain, promotion of non-divine cultural beliefs and practices, and a "negative" domain influence that instills "resistance" to that domain. This lack of domain purity can hamper the promotion of the core domain; however, it also has advantages, such as allowing the Demiurge to increase the expression of domains outside the scope of their leige Luminaries or to promote cultural beliefs and practices that may ultimately lead to mundane events the Demiurge would like to spur on.
### Learning Imagines

A Demiurge starts off with a number of Imagines already known. The greater number of these are derived from the core domains of their leige Luminaries, with a few others associated with other domains with a high enough "similarity" rating to their leige Luminaries' domains. The Demiurge could start with others beyond this, but the actual starting list is determined by the scenario setup.

Demiurges have a few ways of learning about new Imagines that they can employ:

#### Research

Demiurges have two ways to explicitly research a domain to unlock Imagines from its associated tree:

1. The Demiurge can perform the Explore Beliefs action (which can be set to ongoing), focused on a single domain, to accumulate points with which they can "buy" access to new Imagines in that domain's tree. This is reliable and risk-free, and it does not impact the action economy—but it's fairly slow. Either way, there is really no reason to not set up an Explore Beliefs action and leave it ongoing.
2. The Demiurge can direct one of their Proxii to perform this research as well. This is typically even slower (unless the Demiurge instructs many Proxii to contribute to researching a single domain, especially if they have a Stronghold with a library) and takes up a Proxius's actions, but it allows for researching multiple domains at once and can be an easy way to increase a Proxius's loyalty.

Both of these can be done simultaneously.

#### Drawing from the Underreal

A Demiurge can "fish" for new Imagines by interacting with the Underreal. This has advantages and drawbacks.

On the positive side, an Imago successfully drawn from the Underreal is not subject to the "tech tree" structure and its prerequisites, so the Demiurge could potentially gain access to very useful, advanced Imagines this way without having to "climb the ladder," so to speak.

On the other hand:

* If successful, the Demiurge has no control over which domain the Imago they draw out is associated with, and the result may not be useful to them.
* Attempting to draw from the Underreal requires an expenditure of Essence, and the probability of success is fairly low unless the Demiurge expends quite a bit of Essence.
* The Demiurge is not able to use or maintain any other Underreal-related actions while attempting to draw out a concept.
* Luminaries and their Heralds generally look poorly on Underreal interactions in general, but this particular action is often seen as egregious.
* As with all Underreal interactions, there is always the risk of disastrous consequences.

#### Through Usage

As a Demiurge uses Imagines (whether through "whispering" them to mortals or directing Proxii to preach about them), they gain trace amounts of "tech points" in the domains positively associated with them (including "rider" domains but to a lesser degree than the core domain). This is extremely slow and not a valid strategy on its own, but it is automatic and does not require focus.

#### Through Events and Dialogue

There may be times when a prominent mortal worshipper or one of your loyal Proxii—perhaps even a friendly Herald!—will present you with an Imago. Naturally, this can't be relied on, but it is a perk of maintaining good relations with mortals.

Also, an offer of an Imago is a common reward when interacting with Vestiges (the exiled spirits of previous Demiurges now confined to the Underreal). Given that interacting with a Vestige is dangerous and often seen by Luminaries as the worst sin a Demiurge can commit, it is best done as discreetly as possible.

### Brainstorming Imagines and Imago Trees

#### Design Philosophy

##### Tree Tiers

To start with, each domain will have an "Imago Tree" of 7 Imagines, in the following structure:

| Tier      | Number | Dependent on...               |
| --------- | ------ | ----------------------------- |
| 1         | 2      | Nothing.                      |
| 2         | 2      | At least one Imago of tier 1. |
| "Left" 3  | 1      | One specific Imago in tier 2. |
| "Right" 3 | 1      | The other Imago in tier 2.    |
| 4         | 1      | All tier 1–tier 3 Imagines.   |


##### Tier Guidelines

 * Tier-1 Imagines will have the weakest core domain promotion but also weak "rider" domain promotion, as well as moderate anti-domain suppression and cultural influence.
 * Tier-2 Imagines will have stronger core domain expression compared to Tier-1 with less anti-domain supression. Mostly, the "rider" domain and cultural influence that are promoted will just be different than at Tier-1 rather than mathematically stronger or weaker.
 * Tier-3 Imagines will have slightly stronger core domain expression but **stronger** "rider" domain expression and cultural influence, with almost no anti-domain suppression.
 * The Tier-4, "capstone" Imago will be a full realization of the governing domain.

Mathematically:

| Tier | Core Domain | Rider Domain | Anti-Domain | Culture |
| ---- | ----------- | ------------ | ----------- | ------- |
| 1    | <0.4        | ~0.1         | ~-0.1       | 0.1–0.2 |
| 2    | 0.5–0.6     | 0.1–0.2      | >-0.06      | 0.1–0.2 |
| 3    | 0.7–0.9     | 0.4–0.5      | >-0.03      | 0.4–0.6 |
| 4    | 1.0         | N/A          | N/A         | N/A     |

##### More Design Philosophy

The religion trait being structurally separate is the right call and simplifies everything else. Once those are internally balanced across the trees, you're free to treat the remaining slots as a clean design problem.

Here's a framework built around the tier structure and your five non-religion categories:

---

**Tier-by-tier slot philosophy:**

**Tier 1** — Two Imagines, foundational expressions. Each should have:

- 1 `religion:` trait (always, handled separately)
- 1 `values:` trait — the most universal, fundamental virtue the concept evokes
- 0–1 `practice:` trait — a basic societal behavior, optional

No `structure:`, `techno:`, or `relations:` at Tier 1. These Imagines are primordial concepts; they shape _character_ before they shape _society_.

**Tier 2** — Two Imagines, developed expressions. Each should have:

- 1 `religion:` trait
- 1–2 `values:` traits (tend toward 2 for richer Imagines)
- 1 `practice:` trait
- Exceptionally: 1 `structure:` trait if the concept is specifically about social organization

**Tier 3** — Two Imagines, specialized branches. Each should have:

- 1 `religion:` trait
- 2 `values:` traits (the branching divergence should show in meaningfully different value packages between left and right)
- 1–2 `practice:` traits
- 1 rare trait from `structure:`, `techno:`, or `relations:` where strongly motivated — not mandatory, but this is the tier where civilization-shaping concepts earn those tags

**Tier 4** — One Imago, pure domain. Consider:

- 1 `religion:` trait, or none — the capstone transcends specific religious expression in some domains (Void, Truth, Silence feel like they could justify no religion tag; Fire, Community feel like they should keep one)
- 0–1 `values:` trait, very spare
- No `practice:` or rarer tags

The Tier 4 should feel qualitatively different from Tier 3 — less _specific_ cultural promotion, more _weight_.

---

**The values distribution problem:**

The audit overrepresentation of patience, tenacity, and humility reflects a "default virtue" habit — they feel appropriate to almost any concept, which is exactly why they proliferate. The practical fix is assigning each `values:` trait a primary domain home and a secondary domain home, beyond which it should appear only rarely:

|Trait|Primary home|Secondary home|
|---|---|---|
|patience|Silence, Water|Void, Memory|
|tenacity|Conflict|Sacrifice, Memory|
|humility|Void, Decay|Water, Growth|
|pragmatism|Order, Secrecy|Mastery, Conflict|
|idealism|Light, Change|Sacrifice, Truth|
|honesty|Truth, Order|Community, Light|
|sincerity|Conflict, Change|Community, Sacrifice|
|wit|Secrecy, Change|Fire, Mastery|
|moderation|Order, Silence|Water, Fire|
|adaptability|Change, Water|Secrecy, Growth|
|ambition|Mastery, Fire|Growth, Conflict|
|erudition|Truth, Mastery|Memory, Light|
|folk_wisdom|Decay, Conflict|Memory, Growth|
|charity|Community, Sacrifice|Light, Growth|
|prosperity|Growth, Fire|Community, Water|
|indulgence|Fire, Change|Growth, Community|

**Charity and prosperity** are the most conspicuously missing. Charity belongs in Community (The Shared Table is an obvious candidate), Sacrifice, and Light. Prosperity belongs in Growth (The Cultivated Field or The Living City) and Fire (The Errant Lover has an abundance angle that could carry it).

---

**The practice distribution problem:**

`practice:` traits should reflect what mortals _do_ as a result of this Imago's influence. The underrepresented ones mostly have clear natural homes:

- `foraging:` — pre-agricultural Growth and Water Tier 1 Imagines; The Eager Seedling and The Steady Current are both good candidates
- `diplomacy:` — Community Tier 2, Truth Tier 2; the Sworn Witness practically demands it
- `commerce:` — Growth Tier 3 right branch (The Living City already has it), Fire Tier 2 or 3
- `conservationism:` — Growth Tier 3 left (already there), Water Tier 3 right, Decay Tier 3 right

A useful rule: each `practice:` trait should appear across at least 3–4 different domain trees, which ensures that multiple paths lead to the same civilizational behavior rather than certain practices being locked to one domain.

---

**A practical editing pass sequence:**

1. First, assign `religion:` traits systematically across all trees and balance them — get this locked before touching anything else
2. Then audit `values:` traits against the primary/secondary home table above and replace every "default virtue" (patience, tenacity, humility) that sits outside its primary or secondary home with a more specific and underrepresented trait
3. Then address `practice:` gaps by identifying Imagines whose flavor text already implies the missing behavior (foraging, diplomacy, etc.) and adding the tag
4. Finally, consider which Tier 3 Imagines have strong enough civilizational implications to earn a `structure:`, `techno:`, or `relations:` tag — probably no more than 20–25% of Tier 3 Imagines total

This sequence means each pass is narrowly scoped, which should make the Imago Editor's balance readout useful as a continuous check rather than an overwhelming list of problems to fix simultaneously.
