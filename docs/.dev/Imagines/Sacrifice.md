# Sacrifice

**Thematic arc:** The offered gift and the discipline of denial → The price that must be paid and the life freely given → Formalized sacred violence and the willing martyr → The Ultimate Offering.

## Tier 1

### The Offered Gift

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The act of giving itself is the first reward.*

**Description:** The first offering asks a question, and the answer shapes every act of worship that follows: if I give this up, will something be given back? 

**Mechanics:** `sacrifice:0.35, growth:0.1, secrecy:-0.1, culture:luminary_worship:0.2, culture:sincerity:0.15`

### The Pious Fast

**Tooltip Blurb:** *To deny the body what it wants is to rehearse for every larger sacrifice that may be required.*

**Description:** What is given up without being taken is the most honest gift. The fast is the offering no one witnesses, which is part of what makes it costly.

**Mechanics:** `sacrifice:0.3, void:0.1, growth:-0.1, culture:luminary_worship:0.1, culture:patience:0.2, culture:moderation:0.15`

## Tier 2

### The Blood Price

**Tooltip Blurb:** *Some costs are fixed; they do not negotiate and they do not accept substitutes.*

**Description:** The blood price is the teaching that the universe keeps accounts. Whether this is cosmically true or simply a story someone found useful is a question that risks the covenant.

**Mechanics:** `sacrifice:0.55, order:0.15, change:-0.05, culture:luminary_worship:0.2, culture:honesty:0.15, culture:pragmatism:0.15`

### The Given Life

**Tooltip Blurb:** *To give one's life is to make the final statement about what one believed was worth more than living.*

**Description:** The martyr does not merely die; they insist on something. The insistence outlives them, which is either the point or the tragedy.

**Mechanics:** `sacrifice:0.5, truth:0.12, secrecy:-0.04, culture:cooperation:0.1, culture:idealism:0.2, culture:sincerity:0.2`

## Tier 3 — Left (requires *The Blood Price*)

## The Ritual Slaughter

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The formalization of violence into ritual is what separates sacrifice from murder in the minds of the practitioners.*

**Description:** From the perspective of what is killed, this distinction is academic. What the altar demands, it receives, and the civilization organized around the altar tends to become very good at demanding.

**Mechanics:** `sacrifice:0.8, mastery:0.45, change:-0.02, culture:hierarchy:0.2, culture:luminary_worship:0.5, culture:pragmatism:0.15, culture:patience:0.1`

## Tier 3 — Right (requires *The Given Life*)

## The Willing Martyr

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The willing sacrifice is an argument that cannot be answered.*

**Description:** The martyr who chooses their death gives everyone who survives a problem: they cannot pretend the cause was not worth dying for. Whether the cause deserved it is a different question that the martyr has made very difficult to raise.

**Mechanics:** `sacrifice:0.75, growth:0.4, secrecy:-0.02, culture:luminary_worship:0.2, culture:idealism:0.5, culture:sincerity:0.2, culture:tenacity:0.1`

## Tier 4 *(requires all previous)*

### The Ultimate Offering

**Tooltip Blurb:** *Sacrifice is the active giving up of something real in exchange for something believed.*

**Description:** The divine concept of Sacrifice itself.
