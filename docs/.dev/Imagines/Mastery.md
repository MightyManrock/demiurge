# Mastery

**Thematic arc:** The fundamental act of craft and the natural gift → Formal commitment and abstract virtuosity → The impossible made real and the life's work completed → The Perfected Form.

## Tier 1

### The Struck Anvil

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The first blow on the metal is not the important one—but neither is the last.*

**Description:** Every minute act of creation is of equal importance, made with the same attention as the first and the last. This is what mastery asks of people and why most do not achieve it.

**Mechanics:** `mastery:0.35, memory:0.1, void:-0.1, culture:sedentism:0.15, culture:tenacity:0.2`

### The Natural Talent

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The gift arrives without asking, but what is done with it afterwards is still an answer.*

**Description:** Mastery either begins or ends somewhere beyond mere talent. The gift is the precondition—not the achievement.

**Mechanics:** `mastery:0.3, light:0.1, decay:-0.1, culture:competition:0.15, culture:idealism:0.2`

## Tier 2

### The Apprentice's Oath

**Tooltip Blurb:** *To commit to a craft is to accept that you will be wrong, slow, and clumsy for longer than seems reasonable.*

**Description:** The oath to become a creator does not make the years shorter; it makes them possible. What is promised is not success but continuation.

**Mechanics:** `mastery:0.55, sacrifice:0.15, change:-0.05, culture:hierarchy:0.15, culture:patience:0.2, culture:tenacity:0.15`

## The Musician's Score

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The score is what remains of the music when the sound is gone.*

**Description:** The composer writes for an audience they will never meet, leaving instructions for how the thing they made can be made again. This is a faith that has nothing to do with gods.

**Mechanics:** `mastery:0.5, memory:0.12, silence:-0.04, culture:ancestor_worship:0.1, culture:erudition:0.15, culture:idealism:0.15, culture:patience:0.15`

## Tier 3 — Left (requires *The Apprentice's Oath*)

### The Flying Machine

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The machine that should not fly and does anyway is the record of someone who refused to accept current possibility as a fixed limit.*

**Description:** The wings are the argument; the flight is the proof. What follows after is a world in which the impossible thing is simply a fact, and everyone else has to adjust their sense of what is possible accordingly.

**Mechanics:** `mastery:0.8, change:0.45, decay:-0.02, culture:science:0.15, culture:ambition:0.4, culture:tenacity:0.2, culture:idealism:0.2`

## Tier 3 — Right (requires *The Musician's Score*)

### The Grand Composition

**Tooltip Blurb:** *The life's work is the thing that could only have been made by the person who lived that life.*

**Description:** Everything learned and survived and practiced arrives at a single act of complete expression. It cannot be improved; it can only be finished.

**Mechanics:** `mastery:0.75, community:0.4, silence:-0.02, culture:sedentism:0.15, culture:erudition:0.2, culture:ambition:0.15, culture:patience:0.2, culture:humility:0.1`

## Tier 4 *(requires all previous)*

### The Perfected Form

**Tooltip Blurb:** *Mastery is the refinement that builds on accumulated experience until the work and the worker cannot be separated.*

**Description:** The divine concept of Mastery itself.

**Mechanics:** `mastery:1.0`
